Description

Lasagna, pasta dish of Italian origin, made with broad often ruffled noodles and a tomato or white sauce. Lasagna, in the singular, is a southern Italian variation of what northern Italians call lasagne, in the plural

Ingredients

  • Ground Meat
  • Tomato Sauce
  • Onion and Garlic
  • Lasagna Noodles
  • Water
  • Mozzarella Cheese

Steps

  1. Brown ground beef (or sausage) with onions and garlic in a skillet.
  2. Stir in tomato sauce, tomato paste, and seasonings (salt, pepper, oregano, basil, etc.).
  3. Simmer for about 30 minutes to thicken the sauce.
  4. Bring a pot of salted water to a boil.
  5. Add lasagna noodles and cook until they are al dente (still slightly firm when you bite into them).
  6. Drain and set aside.
  7. In a bowl, combine ricotta cheese, eggs, Parmesan cheese, parsley, and seasonings.
  8. Lightly grease a baking dish.
  9. Start with a layer of sauce at the bottom of the pan.
  10. Add a layer of noodles, followed by the cheese mixture, and then another layer of sauce.
  11. Repeat the layers until you have used up all the ingredients, ending with a layer of sauce.
  12. Sprinkle the top with shredded mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.
  13. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  14. Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for about 30 minutes.
  15. Remove the foil and bake for another 15-20 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  16. Let the lasagna rest for at least 10 minutes before cutting and serving.
